Short bio

I am Cristóbal Barba González member of the KHAOS Research Team, I received my PhD in Computer Science (November 2018) from the University of Málaga (Spain), and I am Assistant Professor at the same university. My main lines of research include Big Data analysis techniques, especially Machine Learning and Deep Learning algorithms, and metaheuristic techniques for the optimization and streaming processing of large volumes of data. All this is applied in different fields, such as the environment, agriculture, bioinformatics, tourism and smart cities. I also focus on developing ontologies in other domains and applications that use Semantic Web technologies. I have published in high-impact journals indexed in JCR and have disseminated my research at many national and international conferences. I am collaborating in the multiobjective frameworks jMetal, and jMetalSP.

My research interests include (but are not limited to):

Big Data analysis Machine Learning Semantic Web technologies Evolutionary algorithms Multiobjective optimization Dynamic multiobjective optimization Multi-criteria Decision Making Scientific Workflow Bioinformatics
